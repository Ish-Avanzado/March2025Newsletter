DepEd's Revamped Senior High School Curriculum

Enhancing Employability of SHS Graduates

The Department of Education (DepEd) is set to implement a major revision of the Senior High School (SHS) curriculum starting in School Year (SY) 2025-2026, aiming to enhance graduates' employability by reducing core subjects from 15 to just five or six. This change will allocate more time for work immersion and on-the-job training, ensuring students gain practical skills aligned with industry needs. To facilitate a smooth transition, a phased implementation approach will be adopted. DepEd is collaborating with industry experts to refine standards in key subjects like English, Science, and Mathematics, making education more responsive to workforce demands. Industry partnerships, such as those with Frontline Business Solutions, play a crucial role in this initiative by providing students with real-world experience in fields like IT, Accounting, and Administrative work, ultimately preparing them for successful careers. Schools interested in partnering with Frontline Business Solutions can explore their work immersion program for enhanced hands-on training opportunities.
